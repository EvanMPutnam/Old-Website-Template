Club Involvement

RIT SPEX

The club I am most involved with on campus is a student faculty research organization called RIT SPEX, or Space Exploration. The goal of this organization is to provide students practical experience with space technology to put them one step ahead in the workforce. We have had several students who have interned at NASA, SpaceX, and Lockheed to name a few companies. You can check them out by going to http://spex.rit.edu/


I have had various levels of involvement with SPEX throughout my time at RIT. I ended up joining my freshman semester and that same semester I led a small team in researching a small communications subsystem to be used in a cube satellite. Then the following semester I continued as the communications team lead and led a group in creating two different antennas which were used to gather data from NOAA weather satellites. In addition to this I started to develop an interest in astronomy, and our astro tracking team, when I wrote software for the automation of our Meade LX200 telescope over a wireless network. For the duration of my sophomore year I moved away from communications and into astronomy where I improved our automation software and wrote code to track low earth orbit satellites. I ended up co-leading the astro tracking team in the Spring of 2018.


I have had a number of other roles within the group. One semester I led a small team in the design of a small satellite based on the designs of Moorhead Universities 50$ Sat. Another semester I was treasurer of the group.
